Why 2D

A particle box is two-dimensional, so this settles to the 2D Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution, f(v) ∝ v·e−mv²/2kT, rather than the 3D textbook form ∝ v²·e−mv²/2kT. The gold curve is the correct 2D target. What carries over exactly to 3D is everything that matters here: it rises from zero, peaks at a most probable speed, trails into a long high-speed tail, and — the real lesson — it emerges purely from random elastic collisions conserving energy and momentum, with no special starting speeds required.
